Kind: captions Language: en How to avoid getting head kick when moving your head. When using head movement in Muay Thai or MMA, the most important thing is to pair with proper footwork and disengagement. Head movement without footwork keeps you in range, which is where high kicks become dangerous. After you slip or move your head, you should be stepping out or angling off to create space. That space gives you the ability to lean back safely if a high kick follows. The second key point is anticipation. Anytime you choose to use head movement against punches, you must assume a high kick may come immediately after. This means your hands should stay disciplined and your posture upright, ready to either lean back or block. If you're too close to lean back, your default response should be a tight high guard or frame rather than trying to move your head again. It's also important to understand that this awareness comes with experience and strong distance management. The more time you spend sparring, the better you'll recognize when you're in kicking range versus punching range, and when head movement is safe to use. As your sense of distance improves, your reactions become automatic and avoiding head kicks become less about thinking and more about instinct. In simple terms, avoiding head kicks while using head movement comes down to creating space with footwork, expecting the kick after punches, and managing distance. Skills that develop with experience.
