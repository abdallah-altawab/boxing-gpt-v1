Kind: captions Language: en From what we learned in his stance, Moses will often box with his weight on the back foot, especially when applying pressure. He prefers what we call controlled pressure. Instead of moving forward in the traditional way, front foot first, then back foot, he moves back foot first, then front foot. This way, he can cut the distance while still being out of range, applying pressure cautiously while controlling distance. In fact, while pressuring, he will often step out of range, only to quickly step back in. He'll land with his weight on the back foot, then push off it and shuffle forward to apply pressure again. This polished footwork and balance allow him to control distance and create angles at different levels. His excellent distance control also allows him to stay in range to counter much more effectively.
