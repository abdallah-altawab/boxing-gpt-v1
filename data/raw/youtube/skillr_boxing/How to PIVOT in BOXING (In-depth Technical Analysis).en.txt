Kind: captions Language: en Pivots are a pretty basic move and are taught in the earlier stages of training. To pivot, you will have to put your weight on the front foot and drive your rear foot in a circular motion to one side or the other. When moving the rear foot backwards, you are pivoting to the outside and when moving it forward, you are pivoting to the inside. The motion is similar to a compass where the front foot is fixed and the other foot circles around it. As mentioned, the weight is on the front foot to be able to move the back foot freely. So, it will often be set up with a slip to the outside for the outside pivot or to the inside for the inside pivot.
