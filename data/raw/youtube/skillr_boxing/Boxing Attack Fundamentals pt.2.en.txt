Kind: captions Language: en A common habit in beginners is to really load up on punches before throwing them. This is called telegraphing and makes your shots that much more predictable. But telegraphing can also come from small details like subtle arm movements or even from facial gestures. If you always make the same facial expression when you are about to throw, for instance, lifting your eyebrows, you're giving your opponent a heads up. Eliminating telegraphs takes a lot of work. You need to study yourself. shadow box in front of a mirror. Film your rounds, then observe your punching mechanics and find the smallest details that could give away your intentions. A great way to hide your shots is by moving in a rhythm, which disguises the punch. You can also hide your punch in your high guard, trying to load up as little as possible to make the shot more unpredictable.
