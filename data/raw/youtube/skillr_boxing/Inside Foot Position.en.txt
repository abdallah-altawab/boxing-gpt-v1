Kind: captions Language: en This position is often underrated. Yes, the outside position is advantageous, but that doesn't mean you can't fight effectively from the inside. In fact, the inside position has its own advantages. &gt;&gt; For example, it's easier to perform certain slips and your lead hand is closer for jabbing. However, as we'll see in practice, there are more factors involved that make jabbing from the inside difficult. It's important to learn to box from this position because your opponents will also fight for outside position and will inevitably succeed at times, especially southpaws who have this movement ingrained in their style. As we'll see later on, you can use this to draw opponents into great counters from the inside, too.
