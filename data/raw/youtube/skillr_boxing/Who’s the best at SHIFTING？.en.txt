Kind: captions Language: en The shifting technique was popularized in the late 1800s by Bob Fitz Simmons who often used it to generate more power and knock out his opponents. To shift means to change, especially unpredictably. In boxing, shifting refers to switch stances unpredictably while transferring your weight in the process. When shifting, you are essentially changing your stance from orthodox to southpaw or from southpaw to orthodox. The mechanics of shifting are similar to a pivot. In a regular pivot, you plant your front foot on the ground, keep your weight there, and circle your back foot around it like a compass. When shifting, you are essentially doing the same thing, but this time switching stances. You pivot to bring your back foot forward so that it becomes your new lead foot while transferring your weight during the attack.
