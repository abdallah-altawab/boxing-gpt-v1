Kind: captions Language: en These sessions won't be competitive or challenging, but they give you space to try things, correct mistakes, and perfect movements without fear of getting punished. However, many boxers make the mistake of staying in their comfort zone or taking advantage of the lower opposition. For example, you won't have to mind your technique or be defensively responsible because your opponent can't or won't counter, and this can make you lazy with your form. Instead, these sessions should be seen as an opportunity to improve or learn tricks. Some of the best technicians in the sport like Floyd Mayweather used lower level sparring partners to polish specific moves such as perfecting the shoulder roll in a low-risk setting. The point is to practice the things you are still uncomfortable or feel unnatural doing because with the other two types of sparring partners, you won't have that same freedom. So don't miss the opportunity.
