BOXING TRAINING PROGRAMMES


# 12-Week Boxing Conditioning Cycle

PHASE 1 (Weeks 1-4): AEROBIC BASE BUILDING
Goal: Build the cardiovascular engine that will power all future high-intensity work.
Roadwork: 5 mornings/week. 3 sessions of 40-min easy runs. 2 sessions of 20-min tempo runs (comfortably hard).
Gym: 3 sessions/week. 3 rounds shadowboxing, 4 rounds heavy bag (moderate), 4 rounds mitts, 2 rounds skip rope, 2 rounds speed bag. Focus on TECHNIQUE.
Strength: 2x/week. Squats 4x6, Romanian deadlift 3x8, press 3x8, pull-ups 3x max.

PHASE 2 (Weeks 5-8): SPECIFIC CONDITIONING
Goal: Convert aerobic base into boxing-specific power-endurance.
Roadwork: 4 mornings/week. 2 steady runs. 2 sprint sessions: 10x100m or hill sprints.
Gym: 4 sessions/week. Heavy bag (hard, combinations), mitts (competition simulation), 3 rounds sparring (technical), double-end bag.
Strength: Shift to power — jump squats, kettlebell swings, med ball throws.

PHASE 3 (Weeks 9-12): PEAK AND TAPER
Week 9: Maintain intensity, reduce volume 15%. Sparring up to 6 rounds.
Week 10: Reduce volume further. 1 hard spar/week, 2 technical.
Week 11: Volume down 40% from peak. Technique work only. Rest.
Week 12: Light movement, bag work only. Sleep 9 hours. Trust the preparation.

# Daily Boxing Gym Session Structure

A well-structured boxing session runs 90-120 minutes.

WARM-UP (15-20 min): 5-10 min jump rope. Joint mobility. 2 rounds easy shadowboxing.

MAIN TRAINING (60-80 min):
3-4 rounds shadowboxing (specific combinations, footwork, game plan).
4-6 rounds heavy bag (first 2 moderate, last 2-4 hard).
4-6 rounds mitts (combinations, reactions, defensive drills).
3-6 rounds sparring (technical or hard, depending on phase).
2 rounds speed bag. 2 rounds double-end bag.

CONDITIONING (10-15 min): Core circuit — planks, rotational crunches, ab wheel.
Neck bridging (important injury prevention — build up gradually).

COOL-DOWN (10 min): Easy shadowboxing. Full-body stretching.

# Beginner Boxing Programme (First 3 Months)

MONTH 1: FOUNDATIONS
Focus: stance, guard, jab, basic footwork.
3 sessions/week. Each session: 15 min jump rope, 3 rounds shadowboxing (jab only, footwork), 3 rounds heavy bag (jab only), 1 round speed bag.
No sparring. Learn to move without crossing feet.

MONTH 2: COMBINATIONS
Focus: 1-2, 1-2-3, body shots, basic defence (slip, roll).
4 sessions/week. Add 3 rounds mitt work. Begin controlled technical sparring (last 2 weeks only, light contact, focus on jab and movement).

MONTH 3: RING CRAFT
Focus: distance management, angles, defensive combinations.
4-5 sessions/week. Increase sparring to 2-3 rounds/session. Begin studying your own technique on video. Add roadwork 3 mornings/week.

KEY PRINCIPLE: Master the jab before everything else. A sharp, deceptive jab makes every other technique easier. Do not rush to advanced combinations.
