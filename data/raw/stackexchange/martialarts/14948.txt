# Which judo throws do require a gi to work properly?

In the context of no-gi Brazilian Jiujitsu, "classic" judo throws are often overlooked in favor of wrestling techniques, although there is certainly an overlap between the two. And it does represent an additional challenge that some/many judo throws require to get a grip on the opponents gi. While throwing can be done in no-gi with variations on grips, e.g., cupping behind the elbow instead of grabbing the cloth, I was wondering which judo throws would absolutely require a gi or at least make the throw a lot easier to execute. I am thinking of techniques like tomoe nage , which seem impossible to me to pull off on a resisting opponent when pulling on shirts is not allowed and cupping under both armpits is also difficult to achieve.
