# Are Omoplatas legal in judo?

Are omoplatas legal in judo? Though they cause pain to the shoulder joint, they "lock" the elbow joint (in a bent position) to achieve this (as in ude-garami and te-gatame ).

## Answer (score: 10)
Yes, omoplatas are considered a variant of ashi-gatame or hiza-gatame . 1 3 The IJF Referee Commission has confirmed their legality on multiple occasions, 1 2 both as a submission, and as a turnover: Lascau: Osaekomi. Demonstrator: *uke taps, tori points at armlock* Lascau: Ippon. Ippon. Demonstrator: [With this movement,] making the kansetsu-waza? [It's ok?] Lascau: Ippon. No problem. Ashi-gatame. Ashi-gatame, combination with rolling, and then osaekomi or ashi-/hiza-gatame, depending how is the hand.
