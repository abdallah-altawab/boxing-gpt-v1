## Answer (score: 2)
I and some of my friends wear rashguards or even just T-shirts under our Gi's during class simply to lessen the amount of exposed skin and the amount of skin-to-skin contact. So far as I know, there are no other advantages. As for the drawback, I am not aware of any other than having a rashguard that will need to be washed along with the GI after class. If it is a looser T-shirt, I have seen hands caught in its collar when going for a gi collar grab, but I have yet to see that provide either a meaningful advantage or a disadvantage in the actual round and a tight-fitting rashguard should not have that issue. I believe that the reason they are banned in gi-competitions is simply tradition and it has not been strictly enforced in the competitions I have attended.

## Answer (score: 2)
Although not your question directly, you did mention in your post "Rashguard are forbidden in IBJJF Gi competition." IBJJF bans rashguards because they get in the way while doing collar chokes. Oftentimes the choker will get a hand full of rashguard when trying to attack the opponent's neck, which makes the choke harder to finish.
